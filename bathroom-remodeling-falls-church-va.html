Falls Church Bathroom Remodeling

What bathroom remodeling in Falls Church actually involves.

The majority of Falls Church bathrooms we renovate are in homes built between 1950 and 1985 — cape cods in the city, brick colonials on the Fairfax County side, split-levels throughout Pimmit Hills and West Falls Church. These bathrooms share the same characteristics: 5x8 or smaller footprint, original cast-iron tub, ceramic tile that's been grouted over multiple times, and ventilation that's either not working or dumping into the attic.

The right approach for most of these is a full gut. Pull everything down to the studs, inspect the subfloor, install proper waterproofing (Schluter Kerdi or Wedi board in the shower), address any galvanized supply lines, and rebuild from scratch. A cosmetic overlay on a compromised substrate is a guaranteed redo in 5–7 years.

For larger primary baths in the western Falls Church neighborhoods — Westmoreland Hills, Beverly Hills, the homes along Leesburg Pike — there's more room to work with and more appetite for heated floors, frameless glass, freestanding tubs and full spa configurations.

  • Full gut-and-rebuild bathroom renovations
  • Custom tile showers with proper waterproofing
  • Tub-to-shower conversions
  • Vanities, mirrors, lighting and fixtures
  • Heated tile floors
  • Frameless and semi-frameless glass enclosures
  • Exhaust ventilation routed to exterior (code-compliant)
  • Plumbing supply line upgrades
