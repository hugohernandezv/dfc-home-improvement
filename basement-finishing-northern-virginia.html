Basement Finishing

Finished basements add usable space and real resale value.

Northern Virginia homes — particularly colonials and split-levels built in the 1970s through 1990s in Fairfax County, Loudoun County and Prince William County — commonly have full-footprint unfinished basements with 8- to 9-foot ceilings. That's 700 to 1,200 square feet sitting empty. At $35–65 per finished square foot depending on scope, a basement finish is one of the best investments in the market.

We handle the full scope: moisture assessment, egress window installation if required for bedrooms, framing, insulation, drywall, electrical, lighting, flooring and all finish work. Permits are required and we pull them — always.

NoVA basement specifics

What Fairfax County and Loudoun basement projects actually involve.

01

Permits are mandatory — and enforced

Fairfax County, Arlington and Loudoun County all require building permits for basement finishing that includes framing, electrical, or plumbing. Unpermitted basement space is flagged in home sales and often has to be permitted retroactively at sale — at significantly higher cost and disruption. We pull the permit, schedule inspections and get the certificate of occupancy.

02

Egress windows for legal bedrooms

Any basement room classified as a bedroom must have an egress window with a minimum opening of 5.7 square feet per IRC code. We see a lot of "bonus rooms" that were built as bedrooms without egress — which creates problems at resale and more importantly is a safety issue. We assess egress requirements upfront and include window well installation if needed.

03

Moisture first, finishing second

Northern Virginia basements, particularly in clay-soil areas of Fairfax and Prince William counties, can have seasonal moisture issues. We assess the foundation and drainage before framing begins. Insulating and drywalling over an active moisture problem creates mold. We address the source or advise you to before we frame a single wall.

04

HVAC extension and load calculations

Finished basement space needs to be on the HVAC system — either extending existing ductwork with an HVAC engineer's load calculation, or adding a mini-split. Existing equipment often can't handle the additional load without a proper assessment. We coordinate with licensed HVAC contractors as part of the project scope.

Process

How a DFC basement finish runs.

01

Assessment

Moisture check, ceiling height measurement, egress evaluation, HVAC assessment, utility clearances. Determine what's buildable and what the permit scope requires.

02

Design and scope

Floor plan layout, room designations, bathroom locations, wet bar or kitchen placement, lighting plan and material selections. Fixed-price proposal.

03

Permit

Submit to Fairfax County (or applicable jurisdiction), pull permit, schedule inspections.

04

Rough work

Egress windows if required, framing, electrical rough-in, plumbing rough-in, insulation and county rough inspection.

05

Finish and close-out

Drywall, paint, flooring, trim, lighting fixtures, bathroom tile and fixtures, wet bar cabinetry, final county inspection, certificate of occupancy.
