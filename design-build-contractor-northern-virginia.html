Design-Build

Why design-build produces better projects than design-bid-build.

The traditional model — hire an architect or designer, get drawings, bid it out to contractors — sounds organized. In practice it creates a gap between what was designed and what was built, because the people who designed it have no financial stake in construction and the contractor who built it didn't own the design decisions. When something goes wrong, there's nobody holding the bag.

In the design-build model we use at DFC, the same team that designs your project also builds it. When we make a design recommendation, we're committing to build it at that price. That alignment eliminates the most common sources of change orders, schedule overruns and quality mismatches in remodeling.

  • Single-contract accountability from design to completion
  • 3D renderings before any demolition begins
  • Fixed-price proposals tied to actual construction scope
  • One project manager throughout — no handoffs

Design-build process

How a DFC design-build project runs.

From the first conversation to the final walkthrough — everything handled by one team with no dropped handoffs.

01

Discovery consultation

We visit your home, understand your goals, lifestyle and budget direction. We assess the space, identify structural or permitting considerations, and give you honest feedback on what's achievable with your budget before you spend a dollar on design.

02

Concept and layout

Floor plan layouts, preliminary scope of work, material direction and budget ranges. We present options at different investment levels so you can make an informed decision.

03

Design development and 3D renderings

Detailed drawings, 3D photoreal renderings, material specifications and selections — tile, cabinetry, countertops, fixtures. You approve every detail before we file a permit.

04

Fixed-price proposal

A detailed scope of work with a fixed price tied to the approved design. No ballpark estimates that grow 30% once you're committed. What we quote is what you pay absent owner-directed changes.

05

Permit and pre-construction

We submit permit applications to Fairfax County, Arlington or applicable jurisdiction. Order long-lead materials. Build the trade schedule. Start date confirmed when permits and materials are in.

06

Construction and delivery

Licensed crews, daily site accountability, regular progress updates, clean job site. Inspections handled. Final walkthrough before final payment. Workmanship warranty.
