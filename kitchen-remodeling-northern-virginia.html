Kitchen Remodeling

What a real kitchen remodel looks like in Northern Virginia.

Most Northern Virginia homes were built between 1965 and 2000. Split-levels in Falls Church, colonials in Vienna, cape cods in Springfield — they all came with the same dated U-shaped or galley kitchen that made sense for 1975 and makes no sense now. Closing off the kitchen from the living space, undersized islands, fluorescent lighting, laminate everything.

We gut it and rebuild it right. That means reconfiguring the layout if the floor plan allows, relocating the island, opening the wall to the dining room, upgrading to quartz or quartzite countertops, running custom or semi-custom cabinetry to the ceiling, adding under-cabinet lighting, tile backsplash, new flooring and refreshed electrical. One team, one contract, one point of accountability.

  • Full gut-to-rebuild kitchen renovations
  • Layout reconfiguration and island additions
  • Custom and semi-custom cabinetry
  • Quartz, quartzite and stone countertops
  • Backsplash tile, flooring, trim and paint
  • Electrical, lighting and plumbing upgrades
  • Appliance coordination and installation

Northern Virginia specifics

What makes NoVA kitchen remodels different from other markets.

Fairfax County, Arlington and Alexandria have distinct permitting rules, housing stock and homeowner expectations. We know them cold.

01

Permits in Fairfax and Arlington move differently

Fairfax County requires permits for any work touching electrical panels, load-bearing walls or gas lines — and inspections are not optional. Arlington has its own building department with 2–3 week review times. We handle every permit and inspection so you're not dealing with county bureaucracy. Projects that skip permits create title problems when you sell.

02

Housing stock: 1960s–1990s construction

Most Falls Church, Vienna and Springfield kitchens sit on slab or crawl-space foundations with original 100-amp panels. When we open walls we routinely find knob-and-tube wiring, polybutylene supply lines and inadequate ventilation. We scope this upfront so you're not hit with surprises mid-project. McLean and Great Falls homes tend to be larger custom builds — different challenges, different scope.

03

Neighborhood expectations drive material selection

A kitchen in McLean or Great Falls warrants Calacatta marble, inset custom cabinetry and Wolf appliances. A kitchen in Centreville or Woodbridge is a different investment calculus — still quality, but optimized for the neighborhood's resale ceiling. We advise you on where to spend and where to pull back so your renovation pencils out.

04

HOA considerations in Fairfax County communities

Reston, Burke, Centreville and other planned communities have HOA architectural review for exterior changes and sometimes interior structural modifications. We pull your HOA docs and flag anything that needs approval before we break ground — not after.

Our process

From consultation to final walkthrough — no handoffs, no confusion.

We manage design, permitting, demolition, construction and closeout as one integrated team. You deal with one person, not a rotating cast of subs.

01

On-site evaluation

We walk the kitchen, assess the existing layout, structural conditions and utility locations. We discuss your goals, your timeline and a realistic budget range. No pressure, no pitch — just honest assessment.

02

Design and material selection

Layout options, cabinet specifications, countertop samples, tile selection, appliance coordination. For larger kitchens we produce 3D renderings so you can see the finished space before demo day.

03

Permit and pre-construction

We pull all required Fairfax County, Arlington or Alexandria permits, order materials and schedule the trade sequence — cabinets, countertops, tile, electrical, plumbing — to minimize your downtime.

04

Construction

Licensed, insured crews work on a defined schedule. Daily site cleanup, regular progress updates and direct access to project management throughout. Inspections are handled without you needing to take a day off work.

05

Final walkthrough and warranty

We go through the finished kitchen line by line before we consider the job done. Any punch-list items are addressed before final payment. Workmanship warranty included.
